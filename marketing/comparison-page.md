## Recall vs. Raycast

Raycast is the gold standard for Mac power users — and for good reason. Its clipboard history is fast, polished, and now includes AI search. But:

- **Raycast is Mac-only.** If you use Windows or Linux, it's simply not an option.
- **AI search requires Raycast Pro** at $8/month. The AI runs in Raycast's cloud, which means your clipboard contents leave your machine.
- **Recall's AI is local.** The ~130MB ONNX model runs on your device. No subscription. No server. No data leaves your machine.

**Choose Raycast if:** You're on Mac and already use it as your launcher — the ecosystem integration is hard to beat.

**Choose Recall if:** You're on Windows or Linux, you value privacy, or you want AI clipboard search without a monthly fee.

---

## Recall vs. Alfred

Alfred is the OG Mac launcher with decades of polish. Clipboard history requires the paid Powerpack (£34 one-time).

- **Alfred has no semantic search.** You must remember the exact text you copied.
- **Alfred is Mac-only.**
- **Alfred's Powerpack is a one-time fee** — better value than a subscription if you'll use it long-term.

**Choose Alfred if:** You're a long-time Mac user who relies on Alfred's broader launcher ecosystem (workflows, file search, etc.).

**Choose Recall if:** You need AI semantic search, you're on Windows/Linux, or you want a free and open source option.

---

## Recall vs. Ditto

Ditto is a solid, free, open-source clipboard manager for Windows — but it's showing its age.

- **Ditto has no AI or semantic search.** Exact text search only.
- **Ditto is Windows-only.**
- **Ditto's UI is dated.** It works, but it doesn't have a modern palette-style interface.

**Choose Ditto if:** You need a dead-simple, proven clipboard manager for Windows with no AI overhead.

**Choose Recall if:** You want to search by meaning, prefer a modern keyboard-first UI, or also use Linux.

---

## Recall vs. CopyQ

CopyQ is the most powerful open-source clipboard manager available. It's cross-platform and highly scriptable.

- **CopyQ has no semantic search.** It has powerful scripting (JavaScript/Python) and regex search, but no AI.
- **CopyQ's UI requires configuration.** It's powerful but not beginner-friendly.
- **CopyQ is actively maintained** and battle-tested.

**Choose CopyQ if:** You want maximum scriptability and don't mind spending time on configuration. It's the right tool for power users who want custom automation.

**Choose Recall if:** You want AI search out of the box, a simpler keyboard-first UI, and auto-categorization without scripting.
