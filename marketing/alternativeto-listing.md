## Full Description

Recall is a clipboard history manager that captures everything you copy and lets you search it by meaning, not just exact text.

Press `Ctrl+Shift+Space` from anywhere, describe what you're looking for in plain language, and Recall finds the right clip — even if your wording doesn't match the original text. The entire find-and-paste loop takes under 5 seconds.

**AI search that stays on your machine.** Semantic search is powered by a bundled ONNX embedding model (BGE Small EN) that runs entirely on-device. No API key required, no account needed, no clipboard data ever leaves your machine. Optional integrations with OpenAI or Ollama are available for users who want cloud embeddings.

**Key features:**
- Captures clipboard history silently with 500ms latency
- Auto-categorizes clips: code, URL, email, phone, color, path, address, number
- Raycast-style instant palette — global keyboard shortcut from any app
- Hybrid search: semantic similarity + BM25 fuzzy, merged via Reciprocal-Rank-Fusion
- Pin, rename, and soft-delete clips with 4-second undo
- Native tray icon, launch at startup, rebindable global shortcut
- Windows 11 Mica blur effect, light/dark themes
- Keyboard-first — full cheatsheet at `?`

**Privacy:** All data stored in local SQLite. Zero telemetry. Zero analytics. Network calls only if you opt in to cloud providers.

**Platforms:** Windows (NSIS installer, MSI), Linux (AppImage, .deb). macOS available from source.

**License:** MIT (free and open source)

### vs. Raycast (Clipboard History)
Raycast clipboard is Mac-only and doesn't include semantic search. Recall is cross-platform (Windows + Linux) with on-device AI search built in. No subscription required.

### vs. Alfred (Clipboard History)
Alfred requires a Mac and a paid Powerpack license for clipboard history. Recall is free, open source, cross-platform, and includes semantic AI search that works offline.

### vs. Ditto
Ditto is Windows-only and text-search only. Recall adds semantic AI search (find by meaning, not exact text) and runs on Linux too.

### vs. CopyQ
CopyQ is powerful but complex to configure. Recall is keyboard-first, ships with AI semantic search out of the box, and has a cleaner palette-style UI.

### vs. Pasta
Pasta is Mac-only. Recall is cross-platform with semantic search.

### vs. ClipMenu
ClipMenu is Mac-only and unmaintained. Recall is actively developed, cross-platform, and includes AI-powered search.

### vs. Clipboard for Windows (built-in, Win+V)
Windows' built-in clipboard history is search-only by exact text and doesn't persist across reboots by default. Recall adds semantic search, persistent history, auto-categorization, and pinning.

### vs. GPaste
GPaste is Linux/GNOME-focused and fuzzy-search only. Recall adds semantic AI search and runs on Windows too.
